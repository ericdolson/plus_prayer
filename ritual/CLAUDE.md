# ritual/ — Physical Ritual & Production Context

Inherits all brand-wide truths from the root CLAUDE.md. This workstream is the
daily physical ceremony: the list is printed, assembled, stitched, wax-sealed,
and ceremonially retired on camera. The ritual is both the content and the
product demo.

## Governing principle
**Every object on camera belongs to the ceremony, not a craft workspace.** This
has driven consistent material choices (e.g. an antique silver ladle with patina
over a modern non-stick kitchen spoon).

**Darker over lighter; antique/aged over bright/modern** — across wax colors,
lighting, and finishes.

## Camera & audio
- **Camera:** Sony A7 IV; Sony FE 90mm f/2.8 Macro G OSS (sole lens — covers
  hero wide through 1:1 macro). Picture Profile Off. Lexar V60 UHS-II SD.
- **Audio:** Røde NT1 5th Gen with isolation ball mount, USB-C into MacBook for
  ASMR surface audio; target peak ~-12dB. (XLR ruled out — A7 IV 3.5mm jack
  can't supply 48V phantom power.)
- **Two-angle locked-off shoot:** wide for list assembly, tighter medium for wax
  pour/stamp. White balance matched at the transition between setups.

## Lighting
- Philips Hue ~2200K, very low brightness, as fill.
- Godox SL60IIBi bi-color LED supplemental, motivated from upper left to match
  the oil lamp position.
- Oil lamp as practical light source (replaces candles for continuity).

## Materials
- Resin-based flexible wax sticks (11mm — not paraffin/hexagon beads), melted
  off-camera with a glue gun into an antique silver-plated ladle (1 5/8" bowl,
  hammered pour lip).
- Pewter wax stamp, 1.75"–2" diameter, "+" design, 2.25mm arm width, 1.5mm
  engraving depth.
- Black nitrile gloves (3.5 mil); charcoal fitted long-sleeve henley.
- Cement-top table (17"×15", 28" tall) as the sacred placement surface.

## 7-day wax color rotation (metallic preferred over solid throughout)
- Sunday — Royal Gold
- Monday — Navy Blue
- Tuesday — Deep Red
- Wednesday — Metallic Garnet Purple
- Thursday — Deep Green
- Friday — Antique Brass
- Saturday — Brown

## Post-production
Adobe Creative Cloud (Premiere Pro, Audition 2026); MacBook Pro.
